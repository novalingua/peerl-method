PEER(L) method

What is it?

  • PEER(L) is a writing method designed for academic writing
  • Meant to be simple but sufficient
  • The components of PEER(L) are
    • Point
    • Evidence
    • Explain
    • Result
    • Link (optional)

What about other methods

  • PEER(L) method may not be the best
  • But it is suitable for students at all levels
  • Focus on simplicity and clarity
  • Other methods is not suitable for beginners
    • Students often don't know why they are good

Analysis

Point

  • Goals
    • Readers can understand the aim of the paragraph before reading
    • Clear point is put at the beginning
  • Ask yourself
    • Can the examiner understand the general idea of the paragraph if all they have is the Point sentence?

Side note

  • Putting your Point sentence at the very beginning of the paragraph is considered good practice
  • This is why First-Last sentence is a viable skimming method in IELTS Reading
    • IELTS reading passages are written in the academic style
    • => Many of paragraphs will have their Point sentence (the main idea) at the beginning

General guidelines

  • Keep it simple
    • Simplicity = Clarity
    • Use Simple sentences
    • Avoid Complex/Compound sentences unless you know what you're doing
  • Your Point is what you will try to prove in your paragraph with supporting sentences

Evidence

  • Provide examples to illustrate your Point
    • Avoid examples that are too specific
      • Specific numbers
      • Specific research
      • Things that happened to you personally
    • Do not make up numbers and figures
  • Good evidence
    • Common sense
    • General knowledge
    • Logical conclusions
    • Hedging
    • Approximations
  • Bad evidence
    • Researchers from the University of Oxford demonstrated that 84.8% of dieters regain the weight they have lost within 3 or 4 months.
  • Good evidence
    • Research has consistently demonstrated that the vast majority of dieters quickly regain the weight they have lost.
  • Bad evidence
    • John Smith, a reasearcher at Harvard, proved that personal time and mental health are directly related.
  • Good evidence
    • Many sociologists argue that there is at least a correlation between personal time and mental health.

Explain

  • Explain how the evidence connects to and supports your inital Point
    • Analyze the evidence
    • Interpret its meaning
    • Demonstrate its relevance
  • Interpretation: What does the evidence suggest or imply?
  • Connection: Clearly state how the evidence supports your initial point.
    • Does it confirm, challenge, or add nuance?

Example

  • Point: Regular exercise is essential for maintaining good health
  • Evidence: Studies have shown that exercise can reduce the risk of chronic diseases like heart disease and diabetes.
  • Explain: This is because exercise helps to improve cardiovascular health, regulate blood sugar levels, and maintain a healthy weight.
    • The Explain step clarifies how the evidence (studies showing the link between exercise and reduced disease risk) supports the main point (the importance of exercise for good health).
  • Point: Computer games, like everything else, have many downsides, especially for children.
  • Evidence: Extended and unsupervised gaming sessions can cause many issues, ranging from physical ones such as nearsightedness and joint pain to psychological ones like addiction.
  • Explain: These problems have the potential to adversely impact the lives of anyone, but they are especially damaging to children in their formative years.
    • The Explain step clarifies how the evidence (the link between gaming and health issues) supports the main point (downsides of computer games).

Result

  • This is your "mini" conclusion for the paragraph
  • The goal is to restate your point after you have proven it
  • (Optional) Expand upon your idea
    • Now that you have shown your reasoning
    • What are the consequences/implications of your Point?

Link

  • Link sentences are optional
    • Your thesis and the point of the next paragraph should be clear on their own
  • You have 2 choices
    • Link your Point with the thesis of the essay
    • Link your current paragraph to the idea of the next
  • The Link sentence should only be written if
    1. You have extra time, OR
    2. You haven't met the word count requirements

Notes

  • The Point sentence should almost always be at the beginning
    • Allow readers to understand your main idea as quickly as possible
  • A paragraph can have multiple Evidence and Explain sentences
    • Beginners should focus on developing 1 to 2 ideas ONLY instead of presenting multiple badly developed ideas
  • The Result sentence should follow your Evidence and Explanation
    • This shows examiners that you can follow ideas to their logical conclusions
  • The Link sentence should be at the end of your paragraph if it links to the next idea
